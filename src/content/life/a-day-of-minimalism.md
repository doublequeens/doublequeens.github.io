I woke up this morning with a single question: What can I remove?

Looking around my desk, I realized I had accumulated so much unnecessary stuff. Old notes, half-finished projects, and tools I haven't touched in months. The physical clutter was creating mental clutter.

## The Decision

I decided to experiment with a radical approach: remove everything that doesn't serve a clear purpose in my life right now. Not forever - just for one week to see how it feels.

## The Process

### Morning Routine

Instead of checking three different todo apps, I now have one simple notebook. One list for the day. That's it.

### Digital Life

I unsubscribed from newsletters I never read. I disabled notifications on apps I don't use. I cleared my inbox of promotional emails.

The result? Mental space. Quiet mind.

## What I Discovered

### Less is More

When I reduce my choices, I appreciate each remaining one more. My favorite music playlist feels curated instead of overwhelming. The few apps I keep are the ones I genuinely enjoy.

### Quality Over Quantity

With fewer commitments, I can invest more time in what matters. Better writing, deeper thinking, more meaningful connections.

## One Week Later

I'm still in the middle of this experiment, but the early results have been surprising:

- Less anxiety about "missing out"
- More focus on creative work
- Better sleep quality
- Greater appreciation for simplicity

I'm not sure if I'll stick with this forever, but it's been a valuable lesson: sometimes, adding more options doesn't mean adding more value.

Sometimes, the best thing you can do is subtract.
